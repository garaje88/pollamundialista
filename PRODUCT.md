# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SaludTools employees (the Colombian health-tech company). They access the app from desktop and mobile during work hours and in personal time throughout the World Cup season (June–July 2026). Their primary job: predict match scores before kickoff, track their standing on the leaderboard, and compete informally with colleagues.

## Product Purpose

A private World Cup 2026 prediction pool for the SaludTools team. Employees sign in with Google, predict the full group-stage fixture (72 matches, 5 pts exact / 2 pts correct winner), and climb a live leaderboard. Success = every willing employee participates and checks back after each matchday.

## Brand Personality

Professional-but-playful. Trustworthy, energetic, Colombian pride. Three words: **confiable, emocionante, nuestro**.

The visual identity rides the intersection of SaludTools corporate (clean whites, blue system colors, Inter typography) and World Cup energy (gold accents, bold condensed display type, flag imagery). Neither pure sports app nor pure SaaS — it should feel like something SaludTools built specifically for its team.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark-navy sports apps (ESPN, Sofascore) — too heavy, wrong context.
- Gambling / betting aesthetics — inappropriate for a company app.
- Generic SaaS dashboards with zero personality — boring, no match-day emotion.
- Cream/sand "warm startup" aesthetics — not SaludTools' palette.

## Design Principles

1. **SaludTools DNA first.** Every screen should look like it belongs inside the SaludTools product family: white surfaces, medium blue system color, Inter body type.
2. **World Cup as the accent layer.** Gold, condensed display type, flags, and score widgets are the fun layer — present but not dominant.
3. **Clarity over cleverness.** Predictions must be enterable in one tap. The leaderboard position must be readable at a glance. Zero confusion about how to participate.
4. **Collegiate competition, not gambling.** Tone is warm and collegial. Points are for pride, not money. Copy should feel like an internal Slack channel, not a betting site.
5. **Mobile-first reality check.** Employees will check standings on their phones between meetings. Every layout must work at 375px.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. All text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast. Score inputs must have visible focus rings. Flag images require alt text. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
